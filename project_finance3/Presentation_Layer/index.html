Case Study 1

Automated Monthly Bank Reconciliation for a Mid-Sized Retailer (NetSuite)

A growing retailer with multiple locations faced delays in month-end closes due to manual bank reconciliations in their NetSuite ERP. Teams downloaded statements and exports, then matched transactions in spreadsheets—typically taking 10–15 hours per account monthly, leading to errors and undetected variances until audits.

We created a custom Node.js backend using Express.js and NetSuite’s SuiteTalk REST API for direct, secure data pulls. We integrated Plaid for bank feeds and built an AI matching engine (TensorFlow.js) that auto-matched 95%+ of transactions based on learned patterns, flagging exceptions intelligently with a clean audit trail.

Rollout included OAuth setup, data mapping and testing, then a secure review dashboard and scaling for multi-account environments. Client-requested tweaks (like custom tolerances) shipped quickly post-launch.

Tech Details

Weeks 1–2: OAuth setup, API integrations, and initial data mapping/testing. Weeks 3–4: Matching algorithm development with ML for patterns; secure React dashboard for reviews and certifications. Weeks 5–6 (for multi-account scaling): Advanced rules and performance tuning. Deployed on DigitalOcean for cost-effective scaling. Post-launch tweaks were deployed in days.

Recommendation

Transition to this tailored automation to achieve near-real-time reconciliations, minimize errors, and redirect team focus to strategic finance.

Impact

Time saved: 120+ hours annually per account (95%+ automation). Estimated savings: $12,000+ per account (at $100/hour loaded cost). Error reduction: ~90%, with full audit trails.

Case Study 2: Real-Time Cash Flow Forecasting for a Professional Services Firm (Integrated with Sage Intacct)

Problem Definition

A services firm manually consolidated AP/AR, payroll, and Sage Intacct data weekly via spreadsheets for cash flow planning. Inconsistencies and delays risked liquidity issues amid variable client payments and economic uncertainty.

Quantitative Analysis

The process took 20+ hours weekly, yielding forecasts with 15–20% variance from actuals—potentially causing overdrafts or missed investments.

Qualitative Factors

Unpredictable billing cycles and external pressures (client delays) made static planning inadequate for proactive management.

Solution & Implementation

Tech Details

Weeks 1–2: API authentication, data mapping, and initial syncs. Weeks 3–4: Forecasting engine build with AI predictions. Weeks 5–6: Interactive dashboards (Chart.js) for 13-week rolling views and what-ifs. Hosted on AWS with automated backups; rapid iterations post-go-live ensured alignment with unique drivers.

Recommendation

Adopt this AI-enhanced tool for accurate, agile forecasting that supports growth without cash surprises.

Impact

Time saved: 260+ hours annually. Estimated savings: $26,000. Forecast accuracy improved 25–30%.

Case Study 3: Advanced Project Forecasting and EAC Management (Integrated with Dynamics 365)

Problem Definition

Project managers in a mid-sized engineering and construction firm relied on manual spreadsheets to compile actual costs, forecast remaining expenses, and calculate Estimate at Completion (EAC). This process consumed 8–12 hours per month per project, often leading to outdated insights, delayed variance detection, and inaccurate revenue recognition under percentage-of-completion methods. Without real-time integration to their ERP (Dynamics 365), forecasts were prone to errors from mismatched data, hindering proactive decision-making and risking budget overruns.

Solution & Implementation

We developed a custom EAC and project planning system that seamlessly integrated with Dynamics 365 to pull real-time actual costs (labor, materials, equipment, subcontractors, and commitments). The core tool empowered project managers to input forward-looking forecasts: planned hours for employees and subcontractors, and costs for equipment, materials, and other direct expenses.

The system automatically loaded indirect costs (e.g., overhead allocations) and applied configurable revenue recognition formulas (such as cost-to-cost percentage of completion) to calculate projected revenue, gross margin, and EAC.

Additional functionalities transformed it into a comprehensive project financial management platform: interactive charts and graphs for trend analysis (cost burn rates, variance over time), side-by-side comparisons of forecasts vs. actuals from Dynamics 365, exception reporting and variance alerts to flag potential overruns early, anomaly detection for unusual spending patterns, commentary sections for narrative explanations, and mobile-accessible dashboards for on-the-go updates.

Built with a Node.js backend for robust ERP integration and data processing, paired with a responsive React frontend for intuitive user interaction.

Tech Details

Weeks 1–2: ERP integration setup (secure API pulls from Dynamics 365 Project Operations data), data mapping for actuals, and initial prototyping of the forecasting interface. Weeks 3–4: Core dashboard development, including EAC calculations, revenue formula engine, graphs, and client feedback iterations for usability. Weeks 5–6: Advanced features rollout (exception alerts, anomaly rules, commentary tools), mobile optimizations, and push notifications for critical variances. Weeks 7–8: User training, performance tuning, and go-live with parallel testing against manual processes.

Impact

Reduced monthly forecasting time from 8–12 hours to under 2 hours per project. Annual time savings: 110+ hours per project. Improved forecast accuracy led to earlier intervention on variances, reducing overruns by an estimated 15–20%. Estimated financial savings: $11,000+ per project annually. Enhanced revenue recognition compliance and overall project profitability through real-time, data-driven insights.

Case Study 4: Automated Financial Statement Generation with Driver-Based Insights and Narrative Explanations (Integrated with SAP)

Problem Definition

A mid-sized manufacturer spent 16–20 hours per monthly/quarterly close cycle manually exporting SAP GL data, mapping accounts to financial statement line items, consolidating adjustments, and drafting management narratives. This error-prone process delayed financial reporting, audit preparation, and strategic decisions. Without structured driver analysis, management reviews often failed to pinpoint the root causes behind variances, limiting actionable insights into revenue and cost performance.

Solution & Implementation

We developed an one-click financial statement generation platform with secure SAP integration to automate report pulls, apply dynamic mappings, and produce fully formatted P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow statements on demand.

The system incorporated advanced driver-based analytics by mapping the company’s revenue recognition formula and identifying key drivers (e.g., sales volumes, pricing, product mix, customer segments). We quantified the relative financial impact of each driver through sensitivity analysis and historical correlations. The same methodology was applied to direct costs (e.g., material usage, labor efficiency, production yields) and indirect costs (e.g., overhead allocation drivers, facility utilization).

Key features included: dynamic, database-stored mappings with version control and compliance checks for audit readiness; impact-weighted variance explanations prioritized by dollar impact on key metrics; historical trend analysis with AI-generated insights; guardrails and validation rules; closed-loop learning to refine assumptions and highlight recurring patterns; AI-assisted narrative insights with action-oriented recommendations; interactive dashboards, audit trails, and one-click export to PDF/Excel.

Built on a Node.js service with serverless deployment for instant, scalable execution.

Tech Details

Weeks 1–3: Secure SAP API connections, dynamic mapping engine development, historical data import, driver identification, and automation testing. Weeks 4–5: On-demand generation features, compliance/validation checks, impact scoring logic, and AI narrative integration with initial guardrails. Weeks 6–7: Closed-loop feedback mechanisms, weekly actionable insights module, audit trail enhancements, and client-specific customizations. Week 8: User training, parallel testing against manual processes, and full serverless deployment.

Impact

Reduced statement preparation time from 16–20 hours to under 2 hours per cycle. Annual time savings: 450+ hours. Accelerated close cycles, improved audit readiness, and enabled proactive management of high-impact drivers. Estimated annual savings: $45,000+ through labor efficiency, faster decisions, and enhanced margin visibility. Elevated reporting from compliance-focused output to a strategic tool delivering weekly actionable insights on revenue and cost levers.

Case Study 5: AI-Powered Financial Statement Analysis and Driver-Based Variance Insights (Integrated with Oracle)

Problem Definition

A fast-growing technology firm spent 12–18 hours per monthly/quarterly close cycle manually reviewing Oracle financial statements, dissecting variances, and preparing management reports. This labor-intensive process often overlooked subtle anomalies, delayed identification of root causes, and provided limited actionable insights into key revenue and cost drivers. Without a structured approach to prioritize variances by financial impact, management struggled to focus on high-leverage items, leading to reactive decision-making and less accurate forecasting.

Solution & Implementation

We developed an AI-powered financial analysis platform with automated Oracle statement extraction and advanced driver-based variance analytics. The system began by mapping the company’s core revenue recognition formula and identifying primary drivers (e.g., billable hours, subscription ARR, utilization rates, pricing tiers). We quantified the relative financial impact of each driver using sensitivity analysis and historical correlations. The same approach was applied to direct labor costs (headcount, rates, overtime, productivity) and indirect costs (overhead bases, facility expenses).

Key features included: impact-weighted variance detection prioritized by dollar impact on revenue, gross margin, and EBITDA; historical trend analysis with AI-generated narratives; guardrails and validation rules for realistic inputs; closed-loop learning via monthly actuals vs forecasts feeding back into the model to refine assumptions and highlight recurring miss patterns; action-oriented weekly plans with recommendations on controllable drivers; interactive visualizations, natural-language variance explanations, trend forecasting, and risk alerts.

Built on a Node.js pipeline for reliable data extraction and processing, with AI prompts generating contextual narratives integrated into intuitive dashboards.

Tech Details

Weeks 1–2: Oracle extraction setup, revenue/cost driver mapping, historical data import, and baseline AI prompts for variance detection. Weeks 3–4: Impact scoring engine, dashboards with narratives/alerts, initial guardrails. Weeks 5–6: Closed-loop feedback, weekly action plan modules, anomaly refinements, client-specific prompt tuning. Weeks 7–8: Training, parallel testing vs manual reviews, deployment with performance monitoring.

Impact

Reduced financial review and reporting time from 12–18 hours to under 4 hours per cycle. Annual time savings: 220+ hours. Improved forecast accuracy and earlier identification of high-impact issues, enabling proactive interventions. Estimated annual savings: $22,000+. Transformed finance from backward-looking reporting to a strategic function delivering weekly actionable insights on revenue and cost levers.

Impact

Reduced manual tracking time by over 90%, saving 320+ hours annually. Achieved 20–30% improvement in DSO. Estimated annual savings: $32,000+. Improved cash flow predictability and strengthened client relationships via proactive communication.

Case Study 7: Driver-Based Budgeting and Advanced Scenario Forecasting (Integrated with Infor)

Problem Definition

A mid-sized manufacturing and distribution company relied on complex quarterly spreadsheet models for budgeting and forecasting. Each cycle required 20–30 hours of manual data entry, formula maintenance, and consolidation across departments, leaving little time for meaningful scenario planning. In a volatile market with fluctuating raw material costs, labor rates, and demand, the lack of flexible what-if capabilities limited agility and led to reactive adjustments.

Solution & Implementation

We built a custom FP&A platform with Infor integration to pull real-time actuals, commitments, and operational drivers. Budgeting shifted to a driver-based model: users input drivers and the system produced detailed P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow projections. It enabled unlimited scenarios, real-time collaboration, AI-assisted baselines, dashboards, rolling forecasts, variance alerts, and validation controls.

Tech Details

Weeks 1–3: Infor integration, historical import, driver engine, validation controls. Weeks 4–5: scenario management and collaboration. Week 6: reporting suite, what-if simulations, AI forecasting, dashboards. Weeks 7–8: training, optimization, parallel testing, deployment.

Impact

Reduced quarterly budgeting time from 20–30 hours to under 8 hours per cycle. Annual time savings: 115+ hours. Improved forecast agility and scenario planning. Estimated annual savings: $11,500+. Reduced errors and improved cross-department alignment.
